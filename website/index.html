Did you know? ‚Äî Real examples from popular keyboard privacy policies

Gboard (Google): may use your typing data to improve spelling correction, next-word prediction, and other Google products. Data may be sent to Google's servers even when Gboard is your active keyboard.

SwiftKey (Microsoft): collects words and phrases you type (excluding passwords in password fields) to build a personalised language model. This data is linked to your Microsoft account and synced across devices.

How to Install & Enable

Up and running in under two minutes

Installing a third-party keyboard on Android is a two-step process: first enable it, then set it as your active keyboard.

1

Download & Install

Tap the button below to download the APK, then open it to install. Android will ask you to allow installs from your browser ‚Äî tap Allow. If Play Protect shows a warning, tap Install anyway.

Download APK
2

Open the App

Launch Privacy Keyboard and tap Enable Keyboard. Android will open the keyboard settings list.

3

Toggle It On

Find Privacy Keyboard in the list and toggle it ON. Android will ask you to confirm ‚Äî tap OK.

4

Select as Default

Go back to the app and tap Select as Active Keyboard. Pick Privacy Keyboard from the system picker.
